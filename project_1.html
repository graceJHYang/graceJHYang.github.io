BCHJA Brochure

Skill

Graphic/Print design

Tools

Indesign, Photoshop

Project Overview

Based on the topic of interest, I was required to design a tri-fold brochure for a festival or an event of my choice. With my interest in the equestrian community, I decided to design for the BCHJA Symposium.

Map

One of the main requirements for the brochure was creating an illustrated map for the event’s location. I referenced google map to create the illustration for accuracy and clarity of the location:

Initial Design

For the design, I tried to follow the design elements from BCHJA’s logo: san-serif font with colours of navy, yellow, and gray.

Revised Design

I decided to revise the final brochure after the submission because I wanted to remove the cut-out design. The original version had a trim to create a pop out of the horse however, I realized it wasn’t presenting the way I imagined once printed physically and it wasn’t adding to the design. Furthermore, I shuffled around the information for better flow and organization. With the new layout I was able to have more negative space between texts and images.

Reflection

I was happy with the overall layout, where I received positive comments from my peers and instructor. From this project, I learned about print design: considering how the information is perceived, design consistency, and converting from digital to physical copy.
