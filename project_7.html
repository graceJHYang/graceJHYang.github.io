About RoS

Rain or Shine (RoS) is a family-run business in BC, passionate about sharing their artisan ice cream. With their motto of making people happy, the business delivers delicious artisan ice creams made of fresh, local ingredients. Since 2013, the store has expanded into 3 locations: Cambie, UBC, and Kits.

The company partnered with many small lower mainland farmers to support the local community and to lower the carbon emissions. Their passion for environment protection is reflected by the store's efforts of creating fewer waste by using compostable packaging. RoS proudly presents their homemade ice cream made with fresh, natural, and sustainable ingredients sourced locally, seasonally, and organically.

Project Overview

The main objective of this project was to better understand the participant group (RoS) and develop a design intervention to help support them.

Design Opportunities

During our initial visits, we observed and interviewed the staff to assess the workflow and identify pain points that occur while communicating detailed information. We created a journey map to visualize and understand the tasks and experiences the customers and stakeholders go through.

While the store was welcoming, the customers experienced frustrations with the lack of visual references and instructions which intern delays the ordering and payment processes. Similarly with the stakeholders, the pain points occurred from lack of visual references of the menu and communicating orders between scoopers.

After understanding the frustrations, we narrowed our opportunity to the theme of communication.

Research Findings

During our earlier process, we considered major types of interaction that occur between employees to understand our stakeholders. We compiled their data into 2 comprehensive and relatable personas to represent our target users for the design concept.

We wanted to address the two focal members: the owner and the scooper. It provided us with the perspective of someone who manages the flow of the store as well as of those who are closely impacted by the in-store activities. Based on the stakeholders, we came up with 3 tech-based design concepts which included: 1)Employee’s daily task update system, 2)Order tracking watch, and 3)Stock update system. Our initial design opportunities were focused towards the store’s goal sustainability.

After receiving feedback, we organized the data collected from our research, observations, and interview notes (with the RoS staff) to develop a more realistic persona to generate our concept ideas upon. We redeveloped 3 personas to represent our target users for the design concept.

A key point in shaping our new design focus was understanding why and how there was a level of unawareness and disparity across general employee knowledge.

Design Iterations

Based on our revised design focus, we came up with 3 new concepts to address lack of accessible information.

Participatory Workshop

In order to narrow down to a specific design, we conducted a participatory workshop with the RoS staff members to get their input on the design opportunities and help identify an intervention that aligned with their values and ideas the employees had for better communication.

Through virtual activities, we discovered key values: 1) Emphasis on how communication keeps the workplace flow smoothly 2) Participants prioritized and valued their internal communication 3) How members would integrate their ideas into existing systems.

Final Design: Madlib Relay

With the final design focus of providing an efficient way for shift leaders (aka. Shift supervisors) to transmit information between one another withing the rotational shifts, our final design concept was Madlib Relay. This was an interactive information board, a support system between shift leaders to pass on day-to-day data in order to help make informed decisions, making teamwork and task division more reliable.

The concept is based on the considerations of the stores space, available materials and a shift leaders needs for day-to-day information. It’s a whiteboard that utilizes wooden dowels to place answer cards on the blanks of the given prompts. Most of these derive similarities to existing systems within the store such as the inventory boards and store menu.

Madlib Relay is a reliable way to communicate tasks and updates between not only the shift leaders but to the production staff and scoopers as well. The board was intended to be placed in a high-traffic employee only area such as the kitchen for easy access and visibility for employees.

Reflection

Working with a real organization helped my whole team gain insight on developing an interaction design project. I developed ways to conduct user research, collect and analyze data through interviews and observations to organize them to create personas, user journey maps and ideate/create/iterate our design concept. Over the 12+ weeks, I learned a lot and gained valuable experience working with the organization (RoS) and gave me the knowledge of interaction design, ux research principles, and ways to apply them. I was proud of the journey the team has gone through to create the final design intervention.
