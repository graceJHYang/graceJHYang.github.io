Slojourn

Slojourn is an ultimate travel guide for backpackers and seekers of pilgrimage who want new opportunities to explore the undiscovered paths of East Asia

Skill

Graphic Design, Ideation, Branding, Print/Web Design

Tools

Indesign, Illustrator, Photoshop

Project Overview

As a semester long project, I was required to create my own publication feature article along with readership, editorial mandate + brand, flat pan, cover page, feature article, and the subscription ads along with web article.

Ideation

The publication I decided to design was a travel magazine, featuring South Korea. I wanted to design a clean, minimalistic magazine without packing the articles small and tight. As a travel magazine, I chose colour schemes of white, blue, black, and golden yellow as the brand colour; the main colour is blue as I wanted to express the nature, specifically the ocean.

The original concept was derived from my home country, where I wanted to create a magazine to showcase the beautiful cities to be travelled in South Korea. Specifically, I wanted to feature hidden hotspots and locations that are not widely known to be explored and experience the culture within. However as focusing on one country can be limiting, I expanded the brand to present East Asia. For this feature, I focused on South Korea.

I sketched few layouts for ideation along with flat plan and the editorial lineup to build upon.

Brand

The name Slojourn was derived from the word sojourn which means a slow journey. My publication holds the two meanings: slow journey and sojourn. The word sojourn defines temporary stay which is my brand’s context.

Readership

I analyzed competitive and popular brands to define a realistic group of audience. Furthermore, I looked into the details such as activities, brands, and countries to specify types of content the magazine will hold.

I defined my audience as 60% women and 40& men where slojourners are trendy, minimalist travelers who are active and open-minded, always looking for new opportunities to explore the roads less traveled for enriching experiences.

Editorial Mandate

Slojourn is designed to inspire young travelers to move away from the fast-paced environment and take a step back to explore new opportunities. The publication features iconic cities as well as the undiscovered paths as an ultimate travel guide for exceptional travel experience.

Final Design

For the final layout, I kept the design clean and simple through out by leaving enough white space and having a consistent layout. The san-serif font was chosen for a modern style with a few serif accents. I tried to present the images big and bold to allow viewers to capture the scenery. Furthermore, the advertisements and the web layout hold the same colour scheme for consistency.

Revised Layouts

After the final submission, I reached out to the professor and discussed how I can make my layouts even better as a real editorial magazine. From here, I decided to alter the layouts for more impact to help each element standout more as well as emphasize the hierarchy. Some of the key changes I made are the cover page and the sidebar, along with some edits in the type elements.

Reflection

The 10+ week long editorial project has given me a journey from step 1 to the final product of a magazine/brand creation. It deepened my understanding of each process such as the order and the steps required before designing the layouts for the articles: Flat plan, Editorial Lineup, layout sketch, branding.

I was able to learn how to organize the contents for the article, editing the texts to fit the layout and condensing the information for a smoother flow. Furthermore, incorporating the text elements along with images to create an effective layout.
